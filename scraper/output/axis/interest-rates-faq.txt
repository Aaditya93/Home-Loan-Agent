# Axis Bank — Interest Rates & FAQs

Here is a summary of Axis Bank Home Loan interest rates, fees, tenure options, and frequently asked questions:

- **Interest Rates**:
  - Starting from 8.00% p.a. onwards.
  - General range: 8.00% - 11.90% p.a.
  - Fixed interest rates start @ 14% p.a. onwards.
  - Rates are dependent on CIBIL score, job profile, customer's credit profile, and other risk factors.
  - Both fixed and floating rates of interest are offered.

- **Loan Amount**:
  - Overall: ₹1 Lakh – ₹5 Crore.
  - Axis Bank Housing Loan: ₹3 Lakh - ₹5 Crore.
  - Axis Bank QuikPay Home Loan: Minimum ₹3 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank Shubh Aarambh Home Loan: Up to ₹30 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank Fast Forward Home Loan: Minimum ₹30 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank Asha Home Loan: ₹1 Lakh – ₹35 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank Super Saver Home Loan: ₹10 Lakh – ₹5 Crore.
  - Axis Bank Home Loan Balance Transfer: Up to ₹50 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank Top-up Home Loan: Up to ₹50 Lakh.
  - Axis Bank 24*7 Top-up Home Loan: ₹20,000 – ₹25 Lakh.

- **Loan Tenure**:
  - Overall: Up to 30 years.
  - Axis Bank Super Saver Home Loan: Up to 20 years (plus 2 years moratorium for partially disbursed loans).
  - Shubh Aarambh, Fast Forward, Asha Home Loan: Minimum 20 years at disbursement, Maximum Up to 30 years.
  - Balance Transfer & Top-up Home Loan: Same as the outstanding home loan tenure.
  - 24*7 Top-up Home Loan: Up to 7 years or residual loan tenure, whichever is lower.

- **Processing Fees & Charges**:
  - **Processing Charges**: Up to 1% of the loan amount, subject to a minimum of ₹10,000 + GST. An upfront processing fee of ₹5,000 is charged at the time of loan application submission.
  - **Prepayment Charges (Floating Rate Loan)**: Nil.
  - **Prepayment Charges (Fixed Rate Loan)**: 2% of the principal outstanding.
  - **Admin Charges**: Nil.
  - **Penal Interest Rate**: 8% p.a. above the applicable interest rate on the overdue amount (Maximum 24% per instance).
  - **Switching Fees (Floating Rate to Fixed Rate)**: 1% on the outstanding principal with a minimum of ₹10,000.
  - **Switching Fees (Fixed Rate to Floating Rate)**: 2% on the outstanding principal amount.
  - **Switching Fees (From Higher Fixed Rate to Lower Fixed Rate)**: 0.5% on the outstanding principal with a minimum of ₹10,000.
  - **Repayment Instruction/Instrument Return**: ₹339 per instance.
  - **Cheque/Instrument Swap Charges**: ₹500 per instance.
  - **Duplicate Statement/Amortization Schedule Issuance Charges**: ₹250 per instance.
  - **Duplicate Interest Certificate Issuance Charges**: ₹50 per instance.
  - **CERSAI Charges**: ₹50 for loans up to ₹5 Lakh; ₹100 for loans above ₹5 Lakh.

- **Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio**:
  - Loan amount up to ₹30 Lakh: Up to 90% of property cost.
  - Loan amount above ₹30 Lakh - ₹75 Lakh: Up to 80% of property cost.
  - Loan amount above ₹75 Lakh: Up to 75% of property cost.

- **Special Home Loan Features & Waivers**:
  - **Axis Bank QuikPay Home Loan**: Allows repayment of higher principal amounts earlier in the loan tenure, reducing EMIs over time.
  - **Axis Bank Shubh Aarambh Home Loan**: Offers 12 EMI waivers (4 EMIs each waived off at the end of the 4th, 8th, and 12th year) for borrowers maintaining a clean repayment track record (no more than 3 instances of dues unpaid for 30 days or more, and not overdue for more than 90 days at any point).
  - **Axis Bank Fast Forward Home Loan**: Provides 12 EMI waivers (6 EMIs at the end of 10 years and another 6 months at the completion of 15 years) for borrowers with a clean repayment track record.
  - **Axis Bank Asha Home Loan**: Includes a waiver of 12 EMIs (4 EMIs waived off at the end of the 4th, 8th, and 12th year) for a clear repayment history.
  - **Axis Bank Super Saver Home Loan**: An overdraft facility where surplus funds can be deposited and withdrawn within a pre-determined limit.

- **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**:
  - **Interest Rate Conversion**: Yes, Axis Bank Home Loan interest rates can be converted from fixed to floating and vice versa. A conversion fee of 1% on the outstanding principal (minimum ₹10,000) is charged for floating to fixed, and 2% for fixed to floating. Switching from a higher fixed rate to a lower fixed rate incurs a 0.5% fee on the outstanding principal (minimum ₹10,000). Conversion is allowed up to 3 times during the loan tenure as per policy effective January 1, 2024.
  - **Prepayment**: Yes, you can prepay your Axis Bank Home Loan. There are no prepayment charges for floating rate loans. For fixed-rate loans, a charge of 2% of the principal outstanding applies.
  - **Loan Purpose**: Home loans can be availed for purchasing/construction of new properties, improvements or extensions to existing properties.
  - **Property Document Return**: Axis Bank returns property documents within 15 days from the date of complete repayment of the home loan.
  - **Loan Insurance**: It is not disclosed whether purchasing home loan insurance is mandatory. Purchasing property insurance through Axis Bank for availing the home loan is not mandatory.
  - **Provisional Certificate**: Provides details of principal and interest components of repayments for the current financial year, including borrower details, loan account number, EMI dates, interest rate, principal adjustments, payment updates, missed payments, and part-payment details.
  - **Accessing Statements/Certificates**: Borrowers can download their home loan statement or interest certificate by logging into Axis Mobile app, through Axis Bank internet banking, or the official website. Alternatively, they can use WhatsApp banking, call customer care, or visit an Axis Bank branch/loan center.
  - **Checking Outstanding Balance**: This can be checked by downloading the home loan statement, sending 'Hi' to Axis WhatsApp Banking Number (+91 70361 65000), through mobile banking (Axis Mobile), or internet banking.
  - **EMI Deduction Method**: Home loan EMIs are deducted monthly through a NACH/SI debit mandate with a signed cancelled cheque from the linked savings account.
  - **Tenure Increase for EMI Reduction**: Axis Bank has not mentioned the facility of tenure increase option for reducing home loan EMI. Many lenders consider such requests as loan restructuring, which can be seen as a negative credit event.
