# Bank of Baroda — Eligibility Criteria

Bank of Baroda's home loan eligibility criteria vary based on the scheme, applicant's age, credit score, business vintage/work experience, and repayment history.

- **General Eligibility:**
  - **Applicant Types:** Resident Indian, Non-Resident Indian, Person of Indian Origin (PIO), Overseas Citizen of India (OCI).
  - **Age:** 21-70 years.

- **Specific Criteria for BOB Home Loan Takeover:**
  - **Age:** 21-70 years.
  - **For Individual Applicants:**
    - Minimum CIBIL Score: 701.
    - At least 12 EMIs already paid on the existing loan.
    - Satisfactory repayment track record.
  - **For Non-Individual Applicants:**
    - An individual applicant/co-applicant must be present in the proposed loan.
    - At least 18 EMIs must have been paid on the existing loan.
    - The firm/company should:
      - Have been incorporated for at least 5 years.
      - Be engaged in business activities for the past 2 years.
      - Be earning cash profits for the past 2 years.
      - Have a positive net worth.
      - Have a satisfactory CIBIL rating.
      - Not have slipped into the SMA2 category for the last 12 months.
      - Not be in the RBI defaulter’s list, ECGC Caution list/SAL, Suit filed/Non-Suit filed CIBIL list, CRILIC, etc.

- **Specific Criteria for BOB Credit Risk Guarantee Fund Scheme for Low Income Housing:**
  - New individual borrowers of housing loans up to ₹8 Lakhs under the Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)/Low Income Groups (LIG) category.
  - Housing unit size up to 430 sq. ft. carpet area.

- **Co-applicant Guidelines:**
  - Adding a co-applicant improves eligibility.
  - Close relatives (spouse, father, mother, son, son's wife, daughter, daughter's husband, brother/sister, brother's wife, sister of spouse, sister's husband, brother of spouse) are accepted as co-applicants.
  - If a co-applicant is not a close relative, they must be a joint owner of the property.
  - The bank considers the spouse's salary if they apply as a co-applicant.

- **Loan Amount Determination:**
  - The loan amount is determined by factors such as age, income, repayment capacity, job stability, assets, and liabilities.

- **Loan Tenure Limit:**
  - The sanctioned loan tenure cannot exceed the applicant's retirement age or 65 years, whichever is earlier, despite a maximum tenure of 30 years.
